Bridges

A bridge allows you to move cryptocurrency from one blockchain network to another. For example, if you hold funds on Ethereum but want to use them on Arbitrum or another chain, a bridge handles that transfer. Always double-check the supported networks before initiating a transfer.

  • Stargate (opens in a new tab)

    A widely used bridge for moving assets natively across multiple blockchain networks.

  • Across Protocol (opens in a new tab)

    A fast and low cost bridge focused on Ethereum and its Layer 2 networks.

  • Hop Protocol (opens in a new tab)

    A straightforward bridge for moving tokens between Ethereum and popular Layer 2 chains.

  • Orbiter Finance (opens in a new tab)

    A lightweight bridge supporting a broad range of EVM compatible networks.

  • deBridge (opens in a new tab)

    A cross-chain bridge and messaging protocol supporting a wide range of networks.

Important:
Always verify you are bridging to the correct network and that the bridge supports your chosen asset. Bridging to the wrong network can result in lost funds.

Security & Safety Tools

Tools for reviewing your wallet activity, revoking risky token approvals, and checking transactions before you sign them. Using these regularly is one of the most effective habits you can build in crypto.

Approval Management

  • Revoke.cash (opens in a new tab)

    Review and revoke token approvals granted to smart contracts. A good habit after any DeFi activity.

  • De.Fi Shield (opens in a new tab)

    A wallet security scanner that checks approvals, asset exposure, and potential risks across chains.

Transaction Simulation

  • Pocket Universe (opens in a new tab)

    A browser extension that simulates transactions and warns you of potential risks before you sign.

  • Fire (opens in a new tab)

    A browser extension that previews what a transaction will actually do to your wallet before confirming.

BSC Focused

  • HashDit (opens in a new tab)

    A Web3 security tool built by the Binance security team, focused on BSC risk detection and scam alerts.

Good habits:
Revoke approvals after interacting with unfamiliar contracts. Always simulate a transaction if something feels off. No legitimate service will ever ask for your seed phrase.
