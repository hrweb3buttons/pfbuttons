🔰 New to crypto? Start here

What is a cryptocurrency? A cryptocurrency is a form of digital money secured by cryptography and recorded on a blockchain — a public ledger maintained by a worldwide network of computers, with no central bank or single company in control.

What is a ticker? A ticker is the short code used to identify a coin — like BTC for Bitcoin, or ETH for Ethereum. Think of it like a stock symbol on a traditional exchange.

What is a stablecoin? A stablecoin is a cryptocurrency designed to always be worth a fixed amount — usually $1 USD. They let you move dollar-equivalent funds at crypto speed and low cost, without the price swings of coins like Bitcoin. USDT and USDC are the most widely used. See the Stablecoins tab for the full list.

Is this list always current? This page reflects the coins currently accepted via NowPayments. The list may change as new coins are added or removed from the platform.

🌐 What are networks — and why do they matter?

A network (also called a blockchain or chain) is the infrastructure a coin travels on. Many coins — especially stablecoins like USDT — exist on several networks simultaneously. The coin's value is the same on every network, but fees, speed, and compatibility differ.

⚠️ This is critical when sending crypto: both the sender and recipient must use the same network. Sending USDT via Tron to an Ethereum-only wallet address, for example, will result in the funds being permanently lost. Always confirm the correct network before sending.

💡 Stablecoins & networks — what you need to know: Every stablecoin listed here is worth approximately $1 USD (or €1 EUR where noted), regardless of which network it runs on. However, you must send on the same network as the receiving address. For example, USDT on Tron (TRC-20) and USDT on Ethereum (ERC-20) are technically different assets — sending to the wrong network address will result in permanently lost funds. When in doubt, ask your exchange or wallet which network to use before sending.
